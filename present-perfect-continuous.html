Present Perfect Continuous

Presente Perfecto Continuo

¿Qué es el Present Perfect Continuous?

Describe una acción que comenzó en el pasado y ha continuado hasta el momento presente, enfatizando la duración o el proceso en sí. A diferencia del Present Perfect, el foco está en la actividad en progreso, no en el resultado.

Estructura

✓ Forma Afirmativa

Sujeto + have/has + been + verbo-ing
  • I have been working since morning.
  • You have been working since morning.
  • He/She/It has been working since morning.
  • We have been working since morning.
  • They have been working since morning.

✗ Forma Negativa

Sujeto + have/has + not + been + verbo-ing
  • I have not been sleeping well.
  • She has not been feeling well.
  • They haven't been studying lately.

? Forma Interrogativa

Have/Has + sujeto + been + verbo-ing?
  • Have you been waiting long?
  • Has she been crying?
  • Have they been training?

Usos del Present Perfect Continuous

⏱️ Duración hasta ahora

Cuánto tiempo lleva algo ocurriendo

I have been studying for 3 hours.

🔴 Causa visible

Resultado visible de actividad reciente

He's tired. He has been running.

🔄 Actividad reciente

Acción que acaba de terminar

She has been cooking. (la cocina huele bien)

📈 Tendencia en curso

Cambio que viene sucediendo

Sales have been improving.

Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

Present Perfect → Resultado / cantidad

Énfasis en lo que se completó

I have written 3 emails. (emails terminados)

Present Perfect Continuous → Proceso / duración

Énfasis en la actividad en sí

I have been writing emails. (proceso activo)

Palabras Clave

for since all day all morning lately recently how long?
