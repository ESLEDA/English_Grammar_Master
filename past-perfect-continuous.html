Past Perfect Continuous

Pasado Perfecto Continuo

¿Qué es el Past Perfect Continuous?

Expresa una acción que estuvo en progreso durante un período de tiempo antes de otro momento o evento en el pasado. Combina la anterioridad del Past Perfect con el énfasis en la duración del Continuous.

Estructura

✓ Forma Afirmativa

Sujeto + had + been + verbo-ing
  • I had been working for hours.
  • You had been working for hours.
  • He/She/It had been working for hours.
  • We had been working for hours.
  • They had been working for hours.

✗ Forma Negativa

Sujeto + had + not + been + verbo-ing
  • I hadn't been sleeping well before the trip.
  • She had not been exercising when she got sick.

? Forma Interrogativa

Had + sujeto + been + verbo-ing?
  • Had you been waiting long before he arrived?
  • Had she been studying before the exam?

Usos del Past Perfect Continuous

⏱️ Duración antes de evento

Cuánto tiempo llevaba ocurriendo algo

He had been running for an hour when it started to rain.

🔴 Causa de un estado pasado

Explica por qué algo estaba así

She was exhausted. She had been working all night.

📖 Contexto narrativo

Trasfondo de una historia en pasado

They had been planning the trip for months.

⛔ Actividad interrumpida

Proceso que se detuvo en el pasado

I had been reading when the lights went out.

Past Perfect vs Past Perfect Continuous

Past Perfect → Resultado / finalización

She had written 5 emails. (emails completados)

Past Perfect Continuous → Duración / proceso

She had been writing emails all morning. (proceso continuo)

Verbos de estado: no usan Continuous

know, love, believe, want → usan Past Perfect simple

She had known him for years. (no: had been knowing)

Palabras Clave

for since before when by the time all day all night
