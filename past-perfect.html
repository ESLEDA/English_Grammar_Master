Past Perfect

Pasado Perfecto

¿Qué es el Past Perfect?

El "pasado del pasado". Se usa para hablar de una acción que ocurrió antes que otra acción en el pasado. Siempre implica una referencia temporal: algo sucedió primero (Past Perfect) y luego vino otro evento (Past Simple).

Estructura

✓ Forma Afirmativa

Sujeto + had + participio pasado (V3)
  • I had worked before lunch.
  • You had worked before lunch.
  • He/She/It had worked before lunch.
  • We had worked before lunch.
  • They had worked before lunch.

✗ Forma Negativa

Sujeto + had + not + participio pasado
  • I had not (hadn't) finished when she arrived.
  • They hadn't eaten before the meeting.

? Forma Interrogativa

Had + sujeto + participio pasado?
  • Had you eaten before you arrived?
  • Had she left when you called?

Usos del Past Perfect

⏮️ Anterioridad

Lo que ya había ocurrido antes de otro evento pasado

She had left before I arrived.

🎬 Narración

Explica el trasfondo de una historia

He was tired. He had worked all night.

😔 3ª condicional

Situaciones hipotéticas en el pasado

If I had studied, I would have passed.

🗣️ Reported speech

Discurso indirecto en pasado

She said she had finished the report.

Línea de tiempo del Past Perfect

1º — Past Perfect (lo que ocurrió primero)

By the time he arrived → she had already left.

2º — Past Simple (lo que ocurrió después)

El Past Simple es el punto de referencia en el pasado

When I got home, they had eaten everything.

Conectores frecuentes

before, after, when, by the time, already, just, never, because

Palabras Clave

already just never before after when by the time because
